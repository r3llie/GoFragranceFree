Why Fragrance Free?

Up to 30% of people report having adverse reactions to a petroleum chemical (petrochemical) named "fragrance". Furthermore, people living with Multiple Chemical Sensitivity (MCS) report having allergy-like reactions after being exposed to very low levels of fragrance found in most everyday products. Up to 72% of asthmatics are also affected. Even if you aren't reactive to these synthetic chemicals, over time they may accumulate ~potentially increasing the rist of developing sensitization. & To make matters worse, these chemicals are made to stick onto fabric, hair & skin ~taking multiple washes to fully remove!

How To Fragrance Free?

  • Don't use products with Fragrance, Perfume, Aroma, Parfume, Poly, PEG listed in their ingredients.
  • Don't wear perfume, aftershave, scented lotion or deoderant.
  • Don't use air fresheners (including care ones) & oder eliminators like Febreze
  • Don't use scented laundry detergent, fabric conditioners & or dryer sheets.
  • Use unscented products!

Product Replacements

  • Laundry Detergent: Baking soda, Dr Bronners unscented, Eco Balls, Bio-D, Sonett Natural, Soap flakes.
  • Fabric Softener: Baking soda, White vinegar, Sonnet fabric conditioner, Bio-D fabric conditioner.
  • Bleach: Ecover laundry bleach, Hydrogen peroxide.
  • Steam Guns are also useful for sanitizing kitchen surfaces & bathrooms, cleaning floors, glass, ovens & upholstery

Pro Tips

  • Check to see if the brand of product you use has an unscented version!
  • Look for: Unscented, Free & Clear, & Fragrance Free when shopping for new products.
  • Warning: Manufacturers can legally use the words Natural, Organic, & Hypoallergenic & still use fragrance. & Furthermore, Unscented, Free & Clear, & Fragrance Free does not ALWAYS mean they are free from petrochemicals!
  • Always check the list of ingredients on the back!
